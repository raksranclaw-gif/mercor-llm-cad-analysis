# The Founder's Free Stack: Building Your Demo Without Breaking the Bank

**Author:** Manus AI

For early-stage founders, the goal is to reach a demo-ready product with as little burn as possible. Before you start paying for LLM tokens or high-tier SaaS subscriptions, there is a massive ecosystem of "credits" and "perks" designed to give you a runway.

Below is an extensive list of critical software and infrastructure available for free to builders in 2026.

---

## 2. Essential Development & Hosting
You don't need a DevOps team to launch a demo. These tools offer "Pro" tiers for free to eligible startups.

*   **[Vercel for Startups](https://vercel.com/startups):** Get free Vercel Pro credits to host your frontend. Ideal for Next.js applications.
*   **[GitHub for Startups](https://github.com/enterprise/startups):** 20 seats of GitHub Enterprise free for the first year, including advanced security and Copilot.
*   **[Cloudflare for Startups](https://www.cloudflare.com/startups/):** $250,000 in credits for workers, security, and networking to ensure your demo is fast and safe.
*   **[Supabase for Startups](https://supabase.com/for-startups):** $1,000 in credits for your database, auth, and storage. The open-source alternative to Firebase.

---

## 3. Product & Demo Preparation
When building a demo, "look and feel" matter as much as the code.

*   **[Figma for Startups](https://www.figma.com/startups/):** Free professional tier for 12 months to design your UI/UX.
*   **[Loom for Startups](https://www.loom.com/startups):** Free Loom Business for 12 months to record high-quality video demos for investors.
*   **[Canva for Startups](https://www.canva.com/startups/):** Free Canva Pro to create pitch decks and marketing assets.
*   **[Storylane](https://www.storylane.io/):** Use their free tier to create interactive, clickable product tours without writing code.

---

## 4. Growth & Operations
Managing your first users and tracking data doesn't have to cost thousands.

*   **[HubSpot for Startups](https://www.hubspot.com/startups):** Up to 90% off for the first year. Even their free tier is robust enough for initial CRM needs.
*   **[Mixpanel for Startups](https://mixpanel.com/startups/):** $50,000 in credits to track how users are actually interacting with your demo.
*   **[Segment for Startups](https://segment.com/industry/startups/):** Free for the first year (up to $50k in credits) to manage your data pipelines.
*   **[Stripe Atlas](https://stripe.com/atlas):** While not "free" (it costs ~$500), it often comes bundled with thousands of dollars in perks from partners like AWS and Google.

---

## 5. The "Secret" Directories
If you want to find more niche perks (e.g., free Notion, Slack, or Intercom), use these community-maintained directories:

1.  **[Credit for Startups](https://creditforstartups.com/):** A comprehensive directory of over $3M in startup credits.
2.  **[Secret](https://www.joinsecret.com/):** A marketplace for startup deals that often has 100% off coupons for popular tools.
3.  **[F6S Perks](https://www.f6s.com/perks):** A massive list of discounts available to any founder who registers on the platform.

### Pro Tip for Founders:
Most of these programs require you to be "early-stage" (usually less than 2 years old and under $5M in funding). If you aren't yet incorporated, many of these perks (like AWS Activate Founders) still offer smaller "self-starter" credits to help you get to that first milestone.
